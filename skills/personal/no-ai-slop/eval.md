# No AI slop eval

Run after the edit. Answer each check pass or fail. Any fail: fix the draft, then re-run.

For detect requests, check only the Detect section.

## Voice and meaning

1. Does the edit preserve the user's point without adding claims, examples, stats, quotes, or opinions?
2. Does it preserve the writer's distinctive vocabulary, cadence, bluntness, humor, uncertainty, digressions, and level of polish?
3. Does it leave strong human sentences alone instead of rewriting them for consistency?
4. Is the cutting proportional to the actual slop, with no aggressive compression that strips character?
5. Would the writer recognize this as their own voice?

## Structure

6. Does the draft lead with what the reader needs while keeping personal setup that adds context, tension, or character?
7. Are points front-loaded where that improves clarity, without forcing every unit into the same shape?
8. Is the writer's structure preserved unless it was hurting the piece, with any reorganization explained in What changed?
9. Are genuinely tangled sentences fixed while clear spoken cadence, fragments, and pace changes remain?

## Sentences

10. Active voice with human subjects where possible, no inanimate thing doing a human verb?
11. Direct verbs instead of inflated verb phrases and is/are avoidance?
12. Concrete facts, names, numbers, mechanisms kept and protected instead of smoothed into generic importance?
13. Banned words, empty adverbs, and filler phrases removed unless quoted as examples or carrying real voice?

## Patterns

14. Binary contrasts, negative listing, rhetorical setups, and throat-clearing openers removed?
15. Faux-insight setups, colon reveals, superficial -ing analysis, fake-strong verbs, synonym cycling, and dramatic fragmentation fixed?
16. Importance puffery and weasel attribution replaced with plain facts and named sources, or flagged when no source exists?
17. Fake-profound kicker deleted rather than rewritten into a better metaphor?
18. Summary-recap ending cut so the piece ends on a concrete point, takeaway, or next action?
19. Formatting slop removed: emoji headings, decorative bold, bullets that should be prose, headers over tiny sections, Title Case headings?
20. Sentence case after colons unless grammar, a proper noun, a title, or code requires otherwise?
21. Em dashes absent (harness rule), with hyphens, commas, or periods doing the work?
22. Pasted-LLM artifacts stripped?

## Final read

23. Does the draft avoid robotic symmetry, repeated sentence shapes, stacked punchy fragments, and forced rule-of-three?
24. Would it sound natural read aloud to a sharp colleague?
25. Could this copy sell any product? If yes, it is still generic. Make it belong to this thing only.
26. Does the output include the full edited draft and a short **What changed** section?

## Detect

27. Is every pattern named with a quoted line and a short fix?
28. Is the draft left unrewritten, unscored, and free of any claim about who or what wrote it?
